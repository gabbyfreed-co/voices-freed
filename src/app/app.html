What is Spelling To Communicate (S2C)?

Spelling to Communicate, or S2C, is a way for nonspeaking and unreliably speaking individuals to communicate by spelling out their thoughts. It starts with the belief that many people understand much more than they are able to show, and that the real challenge is often motor-based rather than cognitive. Through supportive lessons and practice, spellers build the purposeful movement skills needed to point to letters on a board and, over time, may move toward typing on a keyboard. S2C gives individuals a way to share their thoughts, feelings, questions, and ideas in their own words.

MOTOR DIFFERENCES

Understanding the Challenge

Many nonspeaking and unreliably speaking individuals understand far more than they can physically express. S2C recognizes that communication challenges are often rooted in motor differences rather than a lack of understanding.

PURPOSEFUL MOVEMENT

Building Reliable Motor Skills

S2C teaches the purposeful motor skills needed to point to letters and communicate through spelling. Lessons are designed to strengthen the connection between thought and action through structured practice and support.

MEANINGFUL COMMUNICATION

Unlocking a Path to Expression

Using a letterboard and eventually a keyboard, individuals can share thoughts, feelings, questions, and ideas in their own words—creating new opportunities for connection, learning, and participation.

WHY I CHOSE S2C

Because every person deserves
a way to express themselves

Hi, Jennifer Freed. I am from Newnan, Georgia and I have spent more than 25 years working alongside individuals with special needs through both the school system and special needs cheerleading programs. Throughout my career, I have been passionate about helping every individual feel seen, supported, and empowered to reach their full potential. My introduction to Spelling to Communicate (S2C) came through learning about the incredible breakthroughs it has created for nonspeaking and minimally speaking individuals. Seeing the communication, confidence, and independence that became possible through spelling was truly life-changing. The more I learned, the more I knew I wanted to be part of helping others access this powerful form of communication. Today, as a trained S2C practitioner, I am honored to support individuals and families as they discover new opportunities for connection, expression, and understanding. I believe every person deserves a way to communicate their thoughts, feelings, and ideas, and I am committed to creating a supportive environment where that can happen. My goal is not only to help individuals build communication skills, but also to encourage and support parents, caregivers, educators, and loved ones as they walk alongside them on that journey. Whether you are just beginning to explore S2C or are looking for additional support, I would be honored to connect with you and your family.
